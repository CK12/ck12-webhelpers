Lesson Summary

  • An equation in which each term is either a constant or a product of a constant and a single variable is a linear equation.
  • Adding, subtracting, multiplying, or dividing both sides of an equation by the same value results in an equivalent equation.
  • To solve an equation, isolate the unknown variable on one side of the equation by applying one or more arithmetic operations to both sides.
